Chain Anchors
What hash-chain verification proves — and what it doesn't: The checks above (Full Verify, Chain Intact) confirm that every record's signature matches its own data and that each record correctly links to the one before it. That proves internal consistency of the data currently stored on this device. It does not prove the data was never altered by someone with access to this device — a rewrite that edits a record and recomputes every hash and link after it will still look perfectly "intact" by those checks alone. An anchor is a small signed snapshot of the chain tip that you save outside this device (download it, email it to yourself, upload it somewhere else). Comparing the chain later against an old anchor can catch a rewrite that the checks above cannot, because the anchor copy isn't somewhere the rewrite can reach.
